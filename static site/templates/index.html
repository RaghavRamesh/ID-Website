Chudar - Shiva's fire

In Hinduism, there are considered to be three cosmic functions - creation, maintenance, and destruction. Each function is said to be the work of each of 3 Gods -Lord Brahma is the creator; Lord Vishnu is the preserver, and Lord Shiva is the destroyer. The name Shiva, in Sanskrit, is an adjective denoting purity and destruction. His cosmic dance is said to have not only consumed ignorance, but also the aging and tired universe, to allow for creation of a new one. He is often seen dancing within an arc of flames. He holds fire in one of his four arms. Fire is considered to be a purifier; in it, all evils are consumed. The fire also represents destruction; complete consumption of the old, to make way for the new - like a phoenix rising out of the ashes.
This relates to another important concept we highlight in this show - the cycle of creation and consumption; the delicate balance between the two that Lord Shiva maintains with his consort, Goddess Parvati. The music for the show is to be composed by famous flautist and composer, Mr B.V. Balasai. The dance items will be choreographed by Mrs Santha Baskar.
